AI: When the "Bug" is You
(Layer 8 Error)

We love laughing when AI draws hands with 7 fingers, but nobody laughs when you leak the company database because you were lazy.

In past articles, we said AI is a hammer. Well, turns out some people are using it to brush their teeth. And when they smash their mouth, they blame the hammer.

Today we are going to talk about what we in cybersecurity call the "Layer 8 Error": the problem located between the chair and the keyboard. That means you. Because AI has no "evil intentions," but your lack of judgment definitely has consequences.

I. The Public Confessional

Let's be real. If ChatGPT is owned by a US corporation (or Chinese, or whatever), why on earth are you pasting your company's source code or your clients' financial data into it for a summary?

There were engineers at Samsung who uploaded confidential code to "optimize it" and basically gifted it to the training model. Golden Rule: If the AI is free, the product is you (and your data).

The Reality: Using a public AI to process private data is like shouting your password in the middle of the town square. It's not "intelligence," it's negligence.

II. The Collective Hallucination

People say: "The AI is hallucinating." No, buddy. AI is a probabilistic model; it predicts the next word that sounds good, not the one that is true.

The one hallucinating is you, believing a chatbot is an encyclopedia validated by a notary. If you ask for a legal reference and it invents a law that doesn't exist (it already happened to real lawyers in the US), the fault isn't on the beta software, it's on the "professional" who didn't verify the source.

III. The Copy-Paste Zombie

We are creating a generation of programmers and creators who know how to paste, but don't know how to read.

The Real Danger

If you copy code from an AI for your banking app and that code has a basic vulnerability you can't detect because "you were too lazy to learn," you are not a developer, you are a public hazard.

Conclusion: Don't Be a "Prompt-Monkey"

AI is amazing for removing the boring part of the job, but it must never replace your final judgment. Use it to draft, but you edit. Use it to code, but you audit.

At Arewa Labs we use AI, sure, but under controlled, local environments when it comes to serious stuff (like in Keyah). We don't give away our intellectual property just to save 5 minutes.

So next time the AI "makes a mistake," ask yourself: Who was behind the wheel?
